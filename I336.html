A domestic and the daughter of Troward Cowell (a horseman) and Ann Dennett, Rose Cowell, the first cousin twice-removed on the mother's side of <a href="I1.html">Nigel Horne</a>, was born in Margate, Kent, England on Jan 21, 1900<span class="citation">1,2,3,4,5</span> and baptized there at St John the Baptist Church on Mar 4, 1900.</p><p>Rose spent all of her life in Kent, England. Throughout her life, she lived in several places around the county: at Gore End Farm, Birchington, Kent, England on Mar 31, 1901<span class="citation">9</span> and on Apr 2, 1911<span class="citation">10</span>; at Granville House, Acol on Jun 19, 1921<span class="citation">11</span>; and on 20 Approach Road in Margate on Feb 16, 1973<span class="citation">8</span>. Rose In 1921 she was working at Henry Woolley Private in Beadon Lodge, Margate.<p>She died on Feb 16, 1973 in Thanet, Kent<span class="citation">3,4,6,7</span>.
