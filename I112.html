James Thomas Lawrence 1893 - 1956

A general farm worker and the son of James Lawrence (an agricultural laborer) and Elizabeth Mount (a domestic servant), James Lawrence, the great-uncle of Nigel Horne, was born in St Lawrence, Thanet, Kent, England on Oct 4, 18931,2,3,4 and married his cousin on his father's side Mary Knowler (with whom he had 5 children: James Thomas, George Edward, Thomas William, Jack and Ronald) at St Augustine, Northbourne, Kent, England on Sep 14, 19185 (JulAug/Sep).

James spent all of his life in Kent, England. Throughout his life, he lived in several places around the county: in Staple on Mar 31, 19016; at Oast Cottages, Cleve, Monkton on Apr 2, 19117; at Meadow Cottages, Marshside on Jun 19, 19218; and at 1 Church Cottage, Woodnesborough on Sep 29, 19392. He served in the military from 1914 to 1915 in France (east Kent Buffs then Labour Corps Awarded the Military Medal). James In 1921 he was working at Mr S Sainford, Farmer at Swinford's Farm, Marshside, Chislet, Kent, England.

He died on Jan 20, 1956 in Ashford, Kent3 (died running for the bus. He had had a heart attack. [ No citation for evidence ]) and was buried at Ramsgate Cemetery, Ramsgate, Kent after Jan 20, 1956.
