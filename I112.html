A general farm worker and the son of James Lawrence (an agricultural labourer) and Elizabeth Mount (a domestic servant)James Lawrence, the great-uncle of Nigel Horne, was born in St Lawrence, Thanet, Kent, England on 4 Oct 18931,2,3,4 and married his cousin on his father's side Mary Knowler (with whom he had 5 children: James Thomas, George Edward, Thomas William, Jack and Ronald) at St Augustine, Northbourne, Kent, England on 14 Sept 19185 (JulAug/Sep).

James spent all of his life in Kent in England. Throughout his life, he lived in several places around the county: in Staple, Kent, England on 31 Mar 19017; at Oast Cottages, Cleve, Monkton on 2 Apr 19116; at Meadow Cottages, Marshside on 19 Jun 19218; and at 1 Church Cottage, Woodnesborough on 29 Sept 19392. He served in the Military from 1914 to 1915 in France (east Kent Buffs then Labour Corps Awarded the Military Medal). James In 1921 he was working at Mr S Sainford, Farmer at Swinford's Farm, Marshside, Chislet, Kent, England.

He died on 20 Jan 1956 in Ashford, Kent, England3 (died running for the bus. He had had a heart attack. [ No citation for evidence ]) and was buried at Ramsgate Cemetery, Ramsgate, Kent after 20 Jan 1956.
