A dock yard laborer and the son of William Dale (an agricultural laborer) and Charlotte Bolding, Thomas Dale, the first cousin four-times-removed on the father's side of Nigel Horne, was born in Chartham, Kent, England on Apr 17, 18541,2,3,4, was baptized there at St Mary on May 6, 1854 and married Jane Laws (with whom he had 5 children: Thomas Henry, Bertha Elizabeth, Lily, Hilda May and Sidney Robert, along with 2 surviving children) at All Saints, Frindsbury, Kent, England on Jan 15, 18818.

Thomas spent all of his life in Kent, England. Throughout his life, he lived in several places around the county: at Heath Farm Cottages, Canterbury on Apr 7, 18611; at Burnt House Farm in Chartham on Apr 2, 187110; at Prentice Street, Strood on Apr 3, 188111; on Montford Road, Strood on Apr 5, 18912; on Brompton Lane, Strood on Mar 31, 190112 and on Apr 2, 191113; and on 13 Glanville Road, Rochester on Sep 29, 19393 which is where he died c. Oct 19395,6,7. He was buried at Old & Cuxton Road Cemeteries, Strood, Kent in Oct 19399.
