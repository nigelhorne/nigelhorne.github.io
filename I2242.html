A general heavy worker foreman and the son of Herbert Horn (a carter) and Mabel Gibson, George Horn, the second cousin twice-removed on the father's side of <a href="I1.html">Nigel Horne</a>, was born in Margate, Kent, England on Mar 8, 1907<span class="citation">1</span>.</p><p>George spent all of his life in Kent, England. Throughout his life, he lived in several places around the county: on 84 Byron Road in Margate on Apr 2, 1911<span class="citation">5</span>; on Iona, Nash Road in Margate on Sep 29, 1939<span class="citation">1</span> (when he was living with his father, Herbert James); at 7 Poet's Corner in Margate in 1959<span class="citation">4</span>; and at 128 Byron Avenue in Margate in 1965<span class="citation">3</span>. He served in the army from 1924 to 1928 (<em>regiment: The Royal Regiment Of Artillery; Service number: 754784</em>) (in 1939, 1939 Civilian Role: Air Raid Precautions).<p>He died <i>c.</i> May 1971 in Thanet, Kent, England<span class="citation">2</span>.
