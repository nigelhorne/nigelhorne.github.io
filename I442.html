A brewer's laborer and the child of John Horn (a laborer) and Mary AnnJames Horn, the three times great-uncle of Nigel Horne, was born in Margate, Kent, England in 18331,2,3,4,5,6,7,8, was baptized there at St John The Baptist Church on Feb 17, 1833 and also married Harriet Fox (with whom he had 10 children: James, Maria L, Rosa Louisa, Isabella, Thomas G, Alice, Charles, Arthur Albert, Emily and Pleasant Alice, along with 1 surviving child) there at St John the Baptist Church, on Apr 21, 18609.

James spent all of his life in Kent, England. Throughout his life, he lived in several places around the county: at Chapel Hill in Margate on Jun 6, 184112 and on Mar 30, 18512; at Hertford Place, Ramsgate, Kent, England on Apr 2, 187111; on Chapel Road, St Lawrence, Thanet on Apr 3, 18815; in Ramsgate in 18916; and at Addington Street in Ramsgate on Mar 31, 19014 - less than a mile from his great-nephew Charles Henry Horne, great-nephew Edmund Horne and nephew Charles Horn who were living at 15 Haine Cottages, St Lawrence, Thanet, Kent, his niece Caroline who was living at 3 Elms Park Terrace in Ramsgate - and his brother William who was living on 62 Boundary Road in Ramsgate -.

He died c. Feb 1906 in Thanet, Kent, England8.
