An agricultural laborer and the child of Thomas Page (a laborer) and Caroline Marsh, Stephen Page, the three times great-uncle of <a href="I1.html">Nigel Horne</a>, was born in Newington, Kent, England in 1817<span class="citation">1,2,3</span>, was baptized in Newington, Hythe, Kent, England on Jan 26, 1817 and married Mary Butcher (with whom he had 7 children: <a href="I2172.html">William Richard</a>, <a href="I2173.html">James</a>, <a href="I3005.html">Charlotte Ann</a>, <a href="I3006.html">Stephen</a>, <a href="I3007.html">Mary</a>, <a href="I3008.html">John</a> and <a href="I3694.html">Sarah Elizabeth</a>, along with 1 surviving child) at Ss Mary & Eanswith, Folkestone, Kent, England on Aug 13, 1837<span class="citation">5</span>.</p><p>Stephen spent all of his life in Kent, England. Throughout his life, he lived in several places around the county: in Folkestone on Jun 6, 1841<span class="citation">6</span>; on Gibraltar Lane in Folkestone on Mar 30, 1851<span class="citation">1</span>; on Bank House, Cheriton Road, Cheriton on Apr 3, 1881<span class="citation">2</span>; and at Bank Cottage, Cheriton in 1900. <p>He died on Jan 2, 1900 in Elham, Kent<span class="citation">3,4</span> and was buried on Folkestone Cemetery, Cheriton Road in Folkestone on Jan 8, 1900<span class="citation">4</span>.
