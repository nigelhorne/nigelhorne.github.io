A waitress and the child of Joseph Dane (a master mariner, licensed victualler) and Emma Stupple (a domestic general servant), Lily Dane, the second cousin twice-removed on the mother's side of Nigel Horne, was born in Faversham, Kent, England c. Feb 19061 and married Ernest Manuel (with whom she had 1 child, Christine) in Wentworth, Ontario, Canada on 24 Apr 1926.

During her life, she was living on Two Brewers, 1 Quay Lane in Faversham on 2 Apr 19112; at 44 South Road in Faversham in 1923 (the same place as her father was living in Nov 1923); and on Dalkeith Avenue, Hamilton, Ontario in Nov 1923. In 1923 she travelled from Quebec, Canada (SS Montclare) to Liverpool, Merseyside, England, departing during Nov, arriving on 25 Oct.

She died on 24 Feb 1968 and was buried at Woodland Cemetery, London, Middlesex, Ontario after 24 Feb 1968.
