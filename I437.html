A policeman and the child of John Horn (a laborer) and Mary AnnWilliam Horn, the three times great-uncle of Nigel Horne, was born in Margate, Kent, England c. 18271,2,3, was baptized there at St John the Baptist's Church on Jul 15, 1827 andhad 4 children with Elizabeth Fox: Thomas W, James John, George Charles and Emma, along with 2 surviving children.

Throughout his life, William lived in several places: at his birthplace in 18411; at Chapel Hill in Margate on Jun 6, 18419; at Batty Street, Whitechapel, London, England on Apr 7, 18618; at 11 Batty Street in Whitechapel on Apr 2, 18717; and on 62 Boundary Road, Ramsgate, Kent, England on Mar 31, 19016 - less than a mile from his great-nephew Charles Henry Horne, great-nephew Edmund Horne and nephew Charles Horn who were living at 15 Haine Cottages, St Lawrence, Thanet, Kent and his niece Caroline who was living at 3 Elms Park Terrace in Ramsgate -.

He died c. Jan 1905 in Thanet, Kent, England3,4 and was buried in Ramsgate on Jan 5, 1905 (Aged 77.)5.
