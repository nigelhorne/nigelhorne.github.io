A fish carrier and the son of Henry Culmer (a coal carman) and Eliza Arnold, Charles Culmer, the first cousin twice-removed on the mother's side of <a href="I1.html">Nigel Horne</a>, was born in Preston, Wingham, Kent, England on Nov 23, 1873<span class="citation">1,2</span>, was baptized there on Jan 11, 1874 and married Elizabeth Hook (with whom he had 2 children: <a href="I2949.html">Charles Frederick</a> and <a href="I2950.html">May Ellen Elizabeth</a>) in St. Mary Cray, Kent, England on Aug 27, 1893<span class="citation">3</span>.</p><p>Throughout his life, he lived on Farwig Lane, Bromley, Kent on Mar 31, 1901<span class="citation">4</span>; and in Hallowell, Prince Edward, Ontario, Canada in 1911<span class="citation">5</span> which is where he died on Oct 6, 1936. He arrived in Canada in 1906.He was buried at Glenwood Cemetery Company, Picton, Ontario on Oct 8, 1936.
