Lily Josephine Manuel (née Dane) cFeb 1906 - 1968

A waitress and the child of Joseph Dane (a master mariner and licensed victualler) and Emma Stupple (a domestic general servant), Lily Dane, the second cousin twice-removed on the mother's side of <a href="I1.html">Nigel Horne</a>, was born in Faversham, Kent, England <i>c.</i> Feb 1906<span class="citation">1</span> and married Ernest Manuel (with whom she had 1 child, Christine) in Wentworth, Ontario, Canada on Apr 24, 1926.</p><p>Throughout her life, Lily lived in several places: on Two Brewers, 1 Quay Lane in Faversham on Apr 2, 1911<span class="citation">2</span>; on Dalkeith Avenue, Hamilton, Ontario in Nov 1923; and on 44 South Road in Faversham in 1923. In 1923 she traveled from Liverpool, Merseyside, England to Quebec, Canada (SS Montclare), departing on 25 Oct, arriving later that same year (sS Montclare.).<p>She died on Feb 24, 1968 and was buried at Woodland Cemetery, London, Middlesex, Ontario after Feb 24, 1968.
