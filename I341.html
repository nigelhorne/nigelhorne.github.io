Emma Culmer (née Beak) 1854 - c1950

Emma Beak, the wife of Charles Culmer (the great-great-uncle of Nigel Horne), was born in Preston, Kent, England on Sep 25, 18541,2,3,4,5,6,7,8,9, was baptized there on Oct 28, 1855 and also married Charles (an agricultural laborer with whom she had 11 children: Henry Gage, Charlotte Ann, Alfred, Charles, William James, George, Fred, Edith Emily, Edward, Arthur Thomas and Georgina Hannah, along with 1 surviving child) there at St Mildred's Church, on Aug 22, 187411 (Jul/Aug/Sep).

Emma spent all of her life in Kent, England. Throughout her life, she lived in several places around the county: at her birthplace in 18611 and in 18712; at The Square, Stourmouth, Kent, England on Apr 3, 188112; in Westmarsh, Kent, England on Apr 5, 189113; at 1 Chalk Hole Cottages, Flete on Mar 31, 190114; at Newports Lydden, Flete on Apr 2, 191115; and at 3 Lydden Farm Cottage, Flete on Sep 29, 19397 following the death of her husband on Jun 21, 1915.

She died c. Aug 1950 in Barnet, London, England10.
