A minister of the methodist church and the 4th of 7 children of Richmond Peters (a farmer) and Caroline Warman, Ovid Peters, the third cousin twice-removed on the father's side of Nigel Horne, was born in Smiths Corner, Kent, New Brunswick, Canada on 12 Jul 18901,2,3, was baptised in Kent, New Brunswick, Canada from 1899 to 1942 and married Pearl Boyd (with whom he had 3 children: Ralph Noble, Phyllis Barbara and Eric Jarvis) in Gagetown, Queens, New Brunswick, Canada on 11 Aug 1915.

During his life, he was living in Weldford, New Brunswick on 28 May 18911; in Elgin, Albert, New Brunswick in 19112; and at Brackley Point Road, Queens, Prince Edward Island, Canada on 1 Jun 19213.

He died on 1 Jul 1934 in Saltcoats, Saskatchewan, Canada and was buried there in 1934.
