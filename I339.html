Edward Culmer 1894 - 1958

A market gardener and the son of Charles Culmer (an agricultural laborer) and Emma Beak, Edward Culmer, the first cousin twice-removed on the mother's side of <a href="I1.html">Nigel Horne</a>, was born in Sarre, Kent, England on Jan 23, 1894<span class="citation">1,2</span>, was baptized in St Nicholas-at-Wade, Kent, England on Mar 18, 1894 and married Hannah Bootes (with whom he had 1 child, Kenneth E) in Thanet, Kent, England around Nov 1925<span class="citation">3</span>.</p><p>Edward spent all of his life in Kent, England. Throughout his life, he lived in several places around the county: at 1 Chalk Hole Cottages, Flete on Mar 31, 1901<span class="citation">6</span>; at Newports Lydden Farm, Flete on Apr 2, 1911<span class="citation">7</span>; and on Lydden Farm, Valley Road, Flete in 1938<span class="citation">4</span>, on Sep 29, 1939<span class="citation">1</span>, in 1953<span class="citation">5</span> and in 1958. <p>He died on Oct 15, 1958 at Grove House Nursing Home, Garlinge, Kent<span class="citation">2</span>.
